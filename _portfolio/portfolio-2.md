Two 1st-order soliton collision can be modeled by the evolution of one 2-nd order soliton, whose eigenvalues have opposite signs. After the collision, they exchange velocity and amplitude, and become time-shifted (compared to the linear propagation case, where they simply propagate through each other as if the other pulse did not exist).
